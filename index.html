SRKey — About & Guide v2.0.0

SRKey helps you generate comprehensive, professional-grade search strategies for systematic reviews using the PICOTS framework — free via Claude.ai, no API key required.

⚠️ Important — Read Before Use

All generated search strategies must be reviewed and validated by a qualified medical librarian before use in a systematic review.

AI-generated queries are a starting point — they may miss database-specific syntax nuances, require additional subject-specific terms, or need adjustment based on your review protocol. Do not use directly without expert validation.

PICOTS framework explained

LetterMeaningExample
PPopulation — who you are studyingAdults with type 2 diabetes
IIntervention — what treatment/exposureSGLT-2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin)
CComparator — what you compare againstPlacebo or standard care (metformin)
OOutcome — what you measureHbA1c reduction, cardiovascular events
TTimeframe — follow-up duration or date range≥6 months follow-up, published 2000–present
SSetting — study context or healthcare settingPrimary care, hospital, community, LMIC
NOTExclusion — what to filter outAnimal studies, in vitro, case reports

Search depth

  • Comprehensive (default) — full synonym lists, truncation (*), MeSH explosion, all brand names, abbreviations, spelling variants. Required for systematic reviews.
  • Quick — core terms only, top 2–3 synonyms per concept, primary MeSH only. Good for scoping reviews or preliminary literature checks.

Search term types

  • MeSH + Title/Abstract (recommended) — NLM MeSH controlled headings + free-text. Broadest coverage — captures both indexed terms and text variants.
  • MeSH only — controlled vocabulary only. May miss newer/unindexed articles.
  • Title/Abstract only — free-text keywords and synonyms. Useful when MeSH headings don't exist yet.
  • Author & journal — generates author names and journal title searches. Use to find all work from a specific group or journal.
  • No term generation — skips term generation; only combined query structure is provided. Use if you have your own terms.
